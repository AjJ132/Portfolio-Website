ProServ

ProServ was conceived to simplify the process of coaches assigning workouts to their athletes. As a sophomore in college when I initiated this project, I had to juggle several factors, including time, cost, and complexity. Balancing my academic and collegiate athlete schedule, I managed to dedicate approximately an hour or two each day to this venture.

The development of ProServ commenced at the start of May and continues as of this writing on July 13, 2023. The project is hosted on Azure, utilizing an ASP.NET backend server and Blazor WebAssembly for the front end. I opted for this configuration due to my affinity for the .NET family and its range of third-party libraries, such as Radzen.

The project incorporates a compact CI/CD pipeline on GitHub, encompassing stages for building, testing, and deploying. As of now, the ProServ mobile iOS app is under development, slated to use the same backend server for its requests.

My experience with Blazor revealed some hurdles, primarily due to its limited support. However, I appreciate the widespread adoption of C#, my primary language. I also found Nuget packages invaluable for accelerating the development process, despite Blazor WebAssembly's relatively high memory usage and increased load times. I'm optimistic about .NET 8 potentially introducing features to enable a hybrid approach to server-side rendering and client-side handling, but this remains speculative.
